# Decision Graveyard

A place to bury your failures so you don't repeat them.

## The Problem

We are obsessed with tracking what we do right. Every project has a roadmap. Every team has a goals tracker. Every person has a task list. But almost nobody keeps a record of what they got wrong.

We forget. We repeat. We make the same mistakes again and again, sometimes years apart, sometimes with different people, always with the same cost.

## The Idea

Decision Graveyard is a simple web app that stores the decisions you made, the assumptions you had, and how they turned out. Nothing more. Just a graveyard of your failed bets.

When you launch a new campaign, build a new feature, or make a business decision, you record it:

**Decision:** Launch Instagram ads to get student signups

**Assumption:** Students spend hours on Instagram and will respond to our ads

**Result:** ₹500 spent, 2 signups

**Lesson:** Students preferred WhatsApp groups. They weren't on Instagram. We should have asked first.

Six months later, your team proposes Instagram ads again. Or two years later, a new team member suggests the same thing. You search Decision Graveyard: "Instagram ads" or "student acquisition" or just "Have I made this mistake before?" and you find your old decision. The assumption. The result. The lesson.

You don't repeat the mistake. You build on it instead.

## Why This Matters

The best companies and individuals learn from their mistakes faster than everyone else. They don't learn faster by being smarter. They learn faster because they write things down. They review them. They share them. They build institutional memory.

Decision Graveyard is that memory.

## What You Can Store

- Bad assumptions that looked good on paper
- Wrong decisions that felt right at the time
- Failed experiments that burned time and money
- Lessons that only cost you once if you remember them
- The context: when it happened, who was involved, what changed since

## Features

- Create a graveyard entry in seconds
- Search your past decisions by keyword, date, or outcome
- Tag decisions by category (marketing, product, hiring, etc.)
- Revisit decisions to add new context or lessons
- Share learnings with your team

## How to Use It

1. Make a decision. Any decision. Launch, build, hire, spend.
2. Write down what you assumed would happen.
3. Wait. See the result.
4. Record what actually happened and what you learned.
5. Move on. But don't forget.

Six months later, when you face a similar choice, search. Find your past self. Learn from them.

## The Philosophy

This is not a tool for perfectionists. It's a tool for people who want to get better. You will fail. You will make mistakes. The difference between people who improve and those who don't isn't intelligence. It's whether they remember their mistakes or forget them.

Decision Graveyard helps you remember.
